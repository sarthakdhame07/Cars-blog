Discontinued Cars That Won't Make It To 2024

Rust in peace to the Dream Cars of the Car Guys

We're nearing the end of 2023 and automakers have already rolled out most of their new products for next year. That
means many of the cars, trucks, and SUVs that we love so much won't make it to 2024.

Luxury brands like Audi and Mercedes-Benz are putting multiple cars to rest as they prep new products,while American
automakers like Chrysler and Dodge have at least one vehicle going the way of the dodo as they prep for the upcoming EV
onslaught.Even a few supercars won’t stick around next year, some destined to be replaced by more powerful models.

More cars will join this list next year. For now, let's say our goodbyes and remember those vehicles we've lost.



:-Audi R8

On the 23rd of March 2024, the last Audi supercar of the ICE age rolled out of Böllinger Höfe. TopGear UK witnessed this imminent heart-wrenching end of an era. The original plan was to kill the R8 in 2023 but Audi held on to this mid-engine V10-powered screamer for one more year. God help us that our generation had to witness the demise of Iron-Man and now the car that has been associated with the fictional yet super-philanthropist.

The R8 was Audi's halo car for decades and was well-appreciated as a practical supercar around the globe. It all started with the Audi Le Mans Quattro concept, and Audi unbelievably pulled it off, doing true justice to the design. Partnering with Audi saved Lamborghini and even ended up in every Punjabi song of that time. So what did Audi get in return? A 5.2-litre V8 engine, even if it was from the humble Gallardo.

Jeremy Clarkson approved that the R8 was the best supercar, without any of the usual drawbacks of a supercar. He even made a hardcore Porsche fanboy like Hammand admit it. Clarkson adored the space and comfort the Audi offered. In particular, the Quattro all-wheel-drive system remained forgiving even during drifts.

Having Lamborghini behind their backs, Audi plonked the V10 from the Huracan right in the middle of the R8. Now the sad reality hits us: the soon-to-retire Huracan is the last standing V10 under these brands.

Through the 17-year-long run, we saw the Audi R8 GT RWD, we saw Audi's adieu in Japan with an extremely rare 8-unit Final Edition in matte Ibis white, and a couple of more limited editions. Now the last of the last Audi R8 is in Vegas yellow, wearing bronze 20-inch wheels.

That said, we are told that this Performance Quattro Edition will neither see a customer nor a red line; instead, it is destined to be showcased in the Ingolstadt Museum. However, this flagship packs a sensational naturally aspirated 5.2-litre V10 producing 612bhp distributed to all four wheels. Even if Audi ever dared to bring back the R8, it would undoubtedly be an electric, hence this is indeed a paradigm shift. One, that we saw coming but weren’t prepared for.


:-Dodge Charger

The modern muscle car Dodge Charger caused a lot of excitement among fans of the original muscle cars when it was introduced in 2005. Why? Because it had four doors. In January 1999, Dodge presented a red concept called at the Detroit Auto Show. The special feature, apart from the natural gas drive, was that the concept had four doors with a coupe-shaped roof line. The car was ahead of its time in terms of alternative propulsion but otherwise quite unemotional so that it was soon forgotten.

In 1983, Dodge unveiled a Shelby Charger modified by none other than racing driver and automotive designer Caroll Shelby himself. Featuring an all-around spoiler package, the Shelby was available only in silver or blue, and had different colored stripes all around and once lengthwise. The 2.2-liter four-cylinder engine received a power boost to 107 horsepower, up from the original 94 horsepower. As a result, the 1983 Shelby Charger made it from 0 to 60 mph in just 10 seconds. Interestingly, due to the heavy modifications, the Charger had to be declared as a Shelby model, and the Dodge trademark did not appear anywhere on the car.

The Dodge Charger is not only the world’s first four-door muscle car, but the 2023 Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat Redeye with a 797-hp 6.2-liter Hemi V8, is also the fastest production sedan in the world, aside from the newly unveiled 2023 Dodge Challenger SRT Demon 170. It does the standing sprint to 60 mph (96 km/h) in just 3.6 seconds and the American quarter-mile in 10.6 seconds at 129 mph (207 km/h), reaching an impressive top speed of 203 mph (326 km/h).

there’s actually no such thing as a Dodge Charger Hybrid. Dodge implemented a clever bit of eye-catching advertising in the ad above in order to make readers aware of the new Charger, which in addition to being a quintessentially American muscle car, features hybrid technology. However, not in the way you expect: instead of saving fuel, it burns both gas and rubber.



:- Chevrolet’s Camaro

The Chevrolet Camaro is one of the most storied and sought-after cars in the history of the automotive industry, and for good reasons. Its power, prestige, features, and options are legendary among all who feel the need for speed. Here, we will dive deeply into the details of the 2023 version of this beast of a coupe.

Eight fantastic trim levels are available for the 2023 Chevrolet Camaro: the 1LS, 1LT, 2LT, 3LT, LT1, 1SS, 2SS, and ZL1. Four extraordinary engines are available to power the 2023 Camaro. The base engine is a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder LTG version granting 275 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The first optional engine is a naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V-6 LGX version providing 335 horsepower and 284 lb-ft of torque.

The second optional engine is a naturally aspirated 6.2-liter V-8 LT1 version generating 455 horsepower and 455 lb-ft of torque. The final optional engine is a supercharged 6.2-liter V-8 LT4 version pounding out an impressive 490 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque. Three transmissions can be paired with these potent engines: a six-speed manual, an eight-speed automatic with paddle shifters, and a 10-speed automatic. Its drivetrain is rear-wheel regardless of trim level.

The Chevrolet Camaro is well-known for leaving the competition in the dust, and the version available for the 2023 model year is no different. The 2.0-liter engine takes this Camaro from zero to 60 in 5.4 seconds. The V-6 does the same in 5.2 seconds. The naturally aspirated version of the V-8 does it in four seconds.

The ultimate engine option, the supercharged V-8, makes the sprint in a blinding 3.5 seconds. Further performance upgrades available through our service center at Vaden Chevrolet of Pooler, Georgia, can lower the time it takes even further, making this Camaro a blur on the track and the open road alike. The 2023 Camaro is rarely utilized for towing but can be fitted to tow up to 1,000 pounds.

It comes standard with 18-inch 5-split spoked silver-painted aluminum wheels, Blackwall all-season tires, power-adjustable body-colored side mirrors, and variable-speed intermittent windshield wipers with a washing unit. Its exterior options include dark tinted tail lamps, heated auto-dimming versions of the side-view mirrors, run-flat versions of the Blackwall tires, front and rear bowties, a stanchion spoiler, and an upgrade to 20-inch bright five-spoke silver-painted aluminum wheels.

:- Porsche Panamera Sport Turismo

The Porsche Panamera is a mid to full-sized luxury car (E-segment or F-segment for LWB in Europe) manufactured and marketed by German automobile manufacturer Porsche[1][2][3] across three generations using a front-engine, rear or all-wheel drive configuration.

As a front-engine, full-size, four-passenger, four-door luxury hatchback weighing nearly 4,000 pounds (1,800 kg), the Panamera runs contrary to the company's historically lightweight two-door, rear-engine sports cars, notably the 911. Though the Panamera's shape and profile recall the 911,[7] where the 911 has a tight, performance-focused interior, the Panamera features commodious dimensions with technological, luxury-oriented amenities.

The engine of the Panamera Turbo S features larger turbochargers than the standard Panamera Turbo's 4.8-litre V8, as well as high-pressure fuel injection, and aluminium-alloy pistons. The piston rings are coated in a low-friction surface to help eke out even more performance. The Panamera Turbo S accelerates to 62 mph (100 km/h) in 3.3 seconds with launch control engaged. Roll-on acceleration should be more impressive, changes to the PDK (Porsche Doppelkupplung) gearbox allow for faster gearshifts, and the alleged 27.7mpg fuel economy is no worse than the less powerful Panamera Turbo.

:- Lamborghini Aventador

The Lamborghini Aventador is a flagship supercar produced by the Italian manufacturer Lamborghini. Here's some key information about the Aventador V12:

1. **Engine**: The Aventador is powered by a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine, which is one of the last remaining naturally aspirated V12 engines in production cars. This engine produces an impressive amount of power, typically around 700 to 770 horsepower depending on the specific variant.

2. **Performance**: With such a powerful engine, the Aventador offers blistering performance. It can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in around 2.9 to 3 seconds, and has a top speed of over 217 mph (350 km/h).

3. **Transmission**: The Aventador typically comes with a 7-speed automatic transmission, which delivers rapid gear changes for seamless acceleration.

4. **Design**: Known for its aggressive and futuristic styling, the Aventador features Lamborghini's signature angular design language. It has sharp lines, large air intakes, and a low-slung profile, making it instantly recognizable on the road.

5. **Chassis and Body**: The Aventador utilizes a lightweight carbon fiber monocoque chassis, which contributes to its impressive performance and handling characteristics. The body panels are also made of carbon fiber, further reducing weight and enhancing aerodynamics.

6. **Features**: Inside, the Aventador boasts a luxurious and driver-focused cockpit, with premium materials and advanced technology. Depending on the variant, it may offer features such as touchscreen infotainment systems, digital instrument clusters, and customizable driving modes.

Overall, the Lamborghini Aventador V12 is a formidable supercar that offers exhilarating performance, breathtaking design, and cutting-edge technology, making it a dream car for enthusiasts and collectors alike.

:-Dodge Hellcat Models

The Dodge Hellcat models refer to a lineup of high-performance vehicles produced by the American automaker Dodge, a subsidiary of Stellantis. The Hellcat models are renowned for their powerful engines and aggressive styling. Here's some key information about the Dodge Hellcat models:

1. **Engine**: The hallmark of the Hellcat models is their supercharged V8 engine. The original Hellcat engine is a 6.2-liter supercharged Hemi V8, capable of producing an astonishing amount of power. In the Challenger Hellcat, it typically generates around 707 horsepower, while in the Charger Hellcat, it offers similar power output.

2. **Performance**: Thanks to the monstrous power output of their engines, the Hellcat models deliver exceptional performance. They are capable of accelerating from 0 to 60 mph in around 3.6 to 4.0 seconds, depending on the specific variant and conditions. The top speed of Hellcat models is electronically limited to around 180 to 200 mph.

3. **Transmission**: The Hellcat models typically come equipped with an 8-speed automatic transmission. This transmission is tuned to handle the immense power of the supercharged V8 engine and provides smooth shifts for both spirited driving and daily commuting.

4. **Design**: The Hellcat models feature bold and aggressive styling cues that set them apart from their counterparts. They often come with unique front fascias, enlarged air intakes, wider fender flares, and other aerodynamic enhancements. The Challenger Hellcat and Charger Hellcat both have muscular and menacing appearances, reflecting their high-performance capabilities.

Overall, the Dodge Hellcat models represent the pinnacle of American muscle car performance, combining jaw-dropping power with aggressive styling and modern amenities. They are beloved by enthusiasts for their exhilarating driving experience and iconic presence on the road.


:- Ferrari F8 Tributo

The Ferrari F8 Tributo is a mid-engine sports car produced by the Italian manufacturer Ferrari. Here's some key information about the F8 Tributo: 1. **Engine**: The F8 Tributo is powered by a twin-turbocharged 3.9-liter V8 engine. This engine is based on the powertrain found in the Ferrari 488 Pista but has been further refined to produce even more power. In the F8 Tributo, it generates an impressive 710 horsepower and 568 lb-ft of torque.

2. **Performance**: With such a powerful engine, the F8 Tributo delivers blistering performance. It can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 2.9 seconds and has a top speed of around 211 mph (340 km/h). It also boasts impressive handling capabilities, thanks to its advanced chassis and aerodynamic design.

3. **Transmission**: The F8 Tributo comes equipped with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. This transmission delivers rapid gear changes for seamless acceleration and enhanced driving dynamics.

4. **Design**: The F8 Tributo features a design that pays homage to some of Ferrari's most iconic models while also incorporating modern aerodynamic elements. It has a sleek and sculpted exterior, with aggressive lines, large air intakes, and a distinctive rear end. The interior is equally impressive, with a driver-focused cockpit and high-quality materials throughout.

5. **Aerodynamics**: Ferrari has placed a strong emphasis on aerodynamics with the F8 Tributo. It features innovative aerodynamic solutions, including S-Duct technology borrowed from the Ferrari 488 Pista, which improves downforce and overall aerodynamic efficiency.

6. **Heritage**: The "Tributo" in the name pays homage to Ferrari's rich heritage, particularly the V8-powered models that have come before it. The F8 Tributo is seen as a celebration of Ferrari's legacy of producing high-performance sports cars with unmatched driving dynamics.

Overall, the Ferrari F8 Tributo represents the pinnacle of Ferrari's mid-engine V8 sports car lineup, offering exhilarating performance, striking design, and cutting-edge technology for enthusiasts and collectors alike.


:-McLaren 720S

The McLaren 720S is a high-performance supercar produced by the British manufacturer McLaren Automotive. Here's some key information about the McLaren 720S: 1. **Engine**: The McLaren 720S is powered by a twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V8 engine. This engine produces an impressive 710 horsepower and 568 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

2. **Performance**: With its powerful engine and lightweight construction, the 720S delivers breathtaking performance. It can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 2.7 seconds and has a top speed of around 212 mph (341 km/h). The 720S also boasts impressive handling characteristics, thanks to its advanced aerodynamics and suspension systems.

3. **Design**: The McLaren 720S features a striking design that is both aerodynamically efficient and visually stunning. Its sleek and sculpted bodywork incorporates innovative aerodynamic elements, including an active rear wing and integrated air intakes. The dihedral doors, which open upwards, are a signature feature of McLaren's supercars.

4. **Variants**: In addition to the standard 720S coupe, McLaren offers several variants of the 720S, including the 720S Spider, which features a retractable hardtop roof for open-air driving. There are also performance-focused variants such as the 720S Track Pack, which includes lightweight components and track-focused enhancements.

5. **Interior**: Inside, the McLaren 720S offers a driver-focused cockpit with a minimalist design and high-quality materials. The seats are comfortable and supportive, and there are various customization options available to tailor the interior to the owner's preferences.

Overall, the McLaren 720S is a remarkable supercar that combines breathtaking performance, cutting-edge technology, and striking design to deliver an exhilarating driving experience. It represents the pinnacle of McLaren's automotive engineering expertise and continues the company's tradition of producing world-class sports cars.
